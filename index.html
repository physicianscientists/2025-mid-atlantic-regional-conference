Purpose

About

The 2025 APSA Mid-Atlantic Regional Conference (MARC) will bring together the regional community of future clinician-scientists of all kinds to build community and learn strategies for success in this career path.

Target Audience

Clinician-scientist trainees at all levels including physician- and veterinarian-scientist trainees as well as undergraduates.

Drew Weissman, MD, PhD

Roberts Family Professor in Vaccine Research, Penn Medicine

Keynote Speaker 1

Dr. Drew Weissman is a Nobel laureate and Director of Vaccine Research in the Infectious Diseases Division at the Perelman School of Medicine as well as Director of the Institute for RNA Innovation at the University of Pennsylvania. His career has focused on RNA and innate immune system biology. Working with Dr. Katalin Karikó, his efforts on mRNA vaccinations paved the way for development of mRNA vaccines for COVID-19 and earned him the 2023 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine. The Weissman Lab continues to investigate mRNA-based technologies as well as vaccination approaches for diseases where vaccine development has previously failed including genital herpes, HIV, HCV, malaria, and tuberculosis. Attendees will have the rare and valuable opportunity to learn firsthand about Dr. Weissman’s career journey which illustrates the value physician-scientists provide to biomedical research.

Susan Volk, VMD, PhD

Corinne R. and Henry Bower Professor of Small Animal Surgery, Penn School of Veterinary Medicine

Keynote Speaker 2

Dr. Susan Volk is the endowed Corinne R. and Henry Bower Professor of Surgery at the School of Veterinary Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania. In addition to practicing clinically as a small animal surgeon, she leads a lab focused on understanding cell and matrix interactions in a variety of oncologic, dermatologic, and orthopaedic contexts. Her lab developed a patented approach to alter tumor microenvironments to prevent metastases for which she won the Penn Center for Innovations’ 2019 Emerging Inventor of the Year Award and the Wound Healing Foundation’s Translational Regenerative Science Award in 2021. Dr. Volk is an exemplary veterinarian-scientist, and trainees will be fortunate to learn about her unique and fruitful career.

Pamela Moalli, MD, PhD

Professor of Obstetrics, Gynecology & Reproductive Sciences, University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine

Keynote Speaker 3

Dr. Pamela Moalli is the director of the Urogynecology and Pelvic Reconstructive Surgery Division at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center Magee-Womens Hospital as well as the Vice Chair of Translational Research at the Magee-Womens Research Institute. She is also a bioengineering professor at the University of Pittsburgh and an adjunct bioengineering professor at Carnegie Mellon University. Her lab focuses on understanding pelvic floor disorders with experimentation and computational modeling as well as designing biomaterials for regeneration of pelvic floor tissues. Clinically, Dr. Moalli practices as surgical urogynecologist and is significantly involved in the training of residents and fellows. As a surgeon and engineer, her career highlights the ways physician-scientists can leverage their skillsets to advance patient care.
